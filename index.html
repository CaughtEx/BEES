Help

Input

Fixed costs - Costs that do not change based on production - this should be the total.

Variable costs 1 - the costs of production for producing the product (per product) before the specified amount of output is reached.

Variable costs 2 - the costs of production for producing the product (per product) after the specified amount of output is reached.

Point of change - the amount of output where variable costs switch from variable cost 1 to variable cost 2.

Selling price per unit - The selling price of the product/service per unit.

Submit - Really?

All inputs for monetary values can be in any currency - so long as it is consistent through all inputs.

Output

Any output that produces 'N/A' is not an error - simply it is where two breakevens do not exist.

Lower BE Point - The (lower) amount of output that is needed to breakeven.

Upper BE Point - The (higher) amount of output that is needed to breakeven.

MEEO - The 'Most Extreme Enclosed Output' is only applicable for two breakevens, it tells you the output needed for the most extreme value in an enclosed area.

MEEPL - The 'Most Extreme Enclosed Profit/Loss' is only applicable for two breakevens, it tells you the highest profit/loss in an enclosed area. Positive values are the largest profit you can make, no matter what, whilst negatives are the largest loss you can make
